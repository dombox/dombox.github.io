Mail Classifications

Let's classify the mails into three categories. (1) Conversational Mails (2) Transactional Mails (3) Promotional Mails

Conversational Mails

You versus another human.

e.g. Mails from friends and family, colleagues etc.

Transactional Mails

You versus a website.

Any mail that's from a website but doesn't contain "Unsubscribe Links"

e.g. Signup Confirmation Mails, Welcome mails, Password Reset, Purchase Receipts etc.

Promotional Mails

You versus a website marketing team.

e.g. Any mail that's from a website but contain "Unsubscribe Links"

Box Groups

Let's classify the boxes into two groups. (1) Normal Mailboxes (2) Isolated Mailboxes

Normal Mailboxes

There is nothing new here.

In our system "Normal Mailboxes" group simply referred as "Mailboxes"

Mailboxes found in this group works exactly like other mail services. e.g. @gmail.com

Address Structure: local-part@domain. e.g. johndoe@domboxmail.com

Although you can use our mailboxes just like other mail services, if you are after "Zero Spam", we highly recommend you to use the "Normal Mailboxes" only for "Conversational Mails".

Isolated Mailboxes

There is something new here

In our system "Isolated Mailboxes" group referred as "Domboxes"

You create a separate mailbox for each domain.

This way even if a website get hacked, the mail address will be useless in the spammers hands. Check "Part 2: The Technology" to understand how we are making your address useless in the spammers hands.

"Domboxes" should be used only for "Transactional and Promotional Mails".

Address Structure: domkey$DomboxDomain@domain

Domkey stands for "Dombox Global keyword". i.e. You set a unique identifier that can be identified in all your domboxes.

You should set your "Domkey" before creating your first dombox. Let's call your domkey "test123"

Now you can create "Dombox" by entering the domain

Yes, we know people are lazy. They are not gonna create a new dombox for every time they signup. This is where our "Teleport" button come in handy. Check our "Part 3: The Contracts" for more info

Address Examples: test123$twitter.com@domboxmail.com, test123$facebook.com@domboxmail.com, test123$linkedin.com@domboxmail.com

Just to be clear, we are not trying to reinvent email. Just because there is a "$" symbol, doesn't mean we reinvented email. "$" symbol is a perfectly valid character in email address.

If "$" symbol freaking you out, we have alternate address structure too as a fallback.

Alternate Address Structure: DomboxDomain@domkey.domain

Address Examples: twitter.com@test123.domboxmail.com, facebook.com@test123.domboxmail.com, linkedin.com@test123.domboxmail.com

Isolated Mailboxes can be put "Offline" or "Deleted" by the user anytime. "Offline" boxes are read-only boxes.

Normal Mailboxes

Let's classify the "Normal Mailboxes" into two types. (1) Primary (2) Mailbox

Primary (P)

This is a "Normal Mailbox". This is the email address you picked while signing up to our service. e.g. giri@domboxmail.com

Mailbox (M)

These are additional "Normal Mailboxes". For most users, there won’t be a need for this box type. Only the “Primary” box is enough.

We can't ask you to immediately switch to our mail service. So you can add your old email account here as a "Mailbox". e.g. johndoe@gmail.com.

In this case our mail service works like a Mail Client found in your mobile. So you can gracefully degrade your old account.

For example if you had already signed up for twitter.com with their old @gmail.com address, you can create a new “Dombox” for twitter.com and then update the email address in their twitter account settings page.

This way you can still use @gmail.com in our mail service, but offload the Transactional and Promotional Mails to our Isolated Mailboxes.

Isolated Mailboxes

Let's classify the "Isolated Mailboxes" into three types. (1) Dombox (2) Combox (3) Hybrid

Dombox (D)

This "Isolated Mailbox" can be created by you manually. You click our "Add Dombox" button, enter the domain and that's it. You have a new dombox. This box type is controlled by you. You can delete these domboxes any time

Combox (C)

These are Domboxes that is under contract. You can create these boxes only via "Teleport" button found in our "Portal Partner" websites

Hybrid (H)

These box types are intended for our "One-Click newsletter subscription" service. These boxes can be created only via "Telescribe" button found in third party websites
