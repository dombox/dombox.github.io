Predestination Paradox

If you ever watched time travel movies, then you already know that the plot of many time travel movies are "Time Loop".

That is whatever happening now, already happened.

For example, You are watching this video right now. Let’s say 5 years from now, someone invents time travel. That person uses the machine and travels back to 10 years. Now you are stuck in a loop. You maybe watching my video for the millionth time.

The time traveller maybe thinking that he is going to change the past by going back. But he changes nothing. This is known as predestination paradox. i.e. Everything is predestined. Even the invention of time machine.

Just think of it like a snake eating its own tail. Many time travel movies follows that principle.

But wait…there is a flaw in that principle.

The Sperm Theory

John is a Canadian who is living in Canada. Chan is a Chinese who is living in China. They both were born in January 1st 1990, exactly the same time. Now ironically, 9 months earlier, both John’s parents and Chan’s parents played the "Mummy-Daddy Game" exactly the same time.

Now let’s say someone uses a time machine and goes back exactly one hour before their parents play the "Mummy-Daddy Game".

If the time traveller goes back to somewhere near John’s parents house in Canada, then there is higher chance John may not be present in the contemporary world. But Chan will be present. He may be living a different life. But definitely he will be here.

The Olive Oil Experiment

I have a bucket of drinking water here. I’m going to put a single drop of olive oil in the centre of this water slowly. Now let’s give some time.

As you can see, it spreads and contaminates the water slowly. It would take some time to spread. But once it's done, the whole water is undrinkable now.

In John’s life, the time traveller triggered such chain of events. Because of such events, John’s parents may or may not play the "Mommy-Daddy Game" on that day. If they play the game, the probability of John being the real John is 1 in billion. That’s because, just like you and me, John is the champion of the sperm race. So this time one of his siblings would win the race.

(A) John may never born. It’s a possibility.

(B) If John was born, the probability of John being the real John is 1 in a Billion. i.e. John might have born as one of his male sibling John, or as one of his female sibling Jane or even as one of his Transgender sibling Joanne.

Chaos Theory

Now let’s come to conclusion.

To change the past, You don’t have to physically go back and kill Hitler. You don’t have to go back to John’s parents house.

All you have to do is that, just transport this 1 mm sandstone back in time and you altered the entire course of the present world.

This is known as "Butterfly Effect".

"The flap of a butterfly's wing in Brazil can set off a cascade of atmospheric events that, weeks later, spurs the formation of a tornado in Texas."

In mathematics, This is what chaos theory is all about. A minor change in the system would yield chaos results.

It's like trying add more cells in the centre of a honeycomb. If you were able to add more cells successfully, then you dislocated the old cells. Right?

You should think of time travel in that perspective. You are not trying to change the past. But trying to dislocate the entire universe.

The only difference with my honeycomb analogy is that, In honeycomb you dislocated the cells only to a certain distance. But in time travel you created Chaos.

1 mm might sound like a very tiny amount. But like I said it can hold 2000 viruses. So the guy who is supposed to get cold and sneeze for a week, is not sick now. The speed of a sneeze is 100 miles per hour. Or should I say, you are moving the air atoms 100 miles per hour when you sneeze. So the chain reaction is altered and it creates a different version of the universe. You get the picture, right?

Now this is what I’m talking about when I say there is a flaw in the predestination paradox. Nothing is predestined.

If you go back in time multiple times to the same period, then that’s like adding a drop of rose essence in a glass of water multiple times. For each time you travel back in time add a drop to the glass of water. The first drop is close to the world as we know it, than the 100th drop.

The Four Pests Campaign

In the late 1950s, Chinese government introduced a campaign called as the Four Pests Campaign

The following four pests must be eliminated. Mosquitoes, Flies, Sparrows and Rats.

Mosquitoes should be eliminated because it spreads Malaria.

Rats should be eliminated because it spreads Plague.

Flies should be eliminated because it spreads Other diseases.

Sparrows should be eliminated because it eats the grains on the paddy fields.

Among these 4 pests, people mostly targeted the sparrows because of its size.

In 2 years they killed massive amount of sparrows.

Here is the problem. The sparrows not only ate the grains. It also ate the insects and worms found in the paddy fields. Since there was no predator, the insects and worms were out of control. It ruined the grains. The ecosystem failed and this created one of the worst famines in history, it's called as the Great Chinese Famine.

According to estimates, this Famine killed 15 to 45 million Chinese people.
